Why Tree Plantation?

Environmental Impact: As a result of their ability to absorb carbon dioxide and produce oxygen through photosynthesis, trees are a key factor in reducing global warming. They serve as organic carbon sinks, lowering greenhouse gas emissions and preventing global warming.

Human Health and Well-being: There are several direct and indirect advantages of trees for human health. By removing pollutants from the air and releasing oxygen, they enhance air quality and create cleaner, healthier surroundings.

Socio-economic Benefits: Plantations of trees may benefit society economically significantly. They produce job openings in a variety of industries, including forestry, landscaping, and tree care services.
